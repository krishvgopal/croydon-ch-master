Direct Recovery from Landlord

I need to advise you, as a landlord who may receive direct payments of Housing Benefit on behalf of your tenants, of an important aspect of Benefit legislation that may apply regarding the recovery of Housing Benefit overpayments.

 

We can recover overpaid Housing Benefit that has been paid to you by reducing the amount of Housing Benefit paid.  To do this we may reduce the amount of benefit paid to you for a tenant who does not have the overpayment.  This is in accordance with Section 16 of the Social Security Administration (Fraud) Act 1997.

 

The Housing Benefit (recovery of overpayments) Regulations 1997 state that the rent liability for the tenant whose benefit has been reduced will be deemed to be paid to the full value of their Housing Benefit entitlement. This means that you must credit the rent account of the affected tenant with the amount of Housing Benefit they were actually entitled to.

 

Recovery by Court Action

I must also inform you that under section 75(7) of the Social Security Administration Act 1992 an application can be made to have your debt of Housing Benefit registered as an order of court. 

 

The County Court will then send you an order to pay and you may have to meet additional costs.  Failure to pay the Court Order means that the debt can then be recovered by any of the following methods:

 

  • Referral to an external Debt Collection Agency;
  • A warrant of execution giving court bailiffs the authority to seize goods;
  • An attachment of earnings order which permits deductions direct from your earnings;
  • A third party debt order which allows payment direct from bank/building society accounts;
  • A charging order which prevents sale of assets unless the debt is cleared.

 

PLEASE NOTE, YOU MAY FIND IT DIFFICULT TO GET CREDIT IN FUTURE IF YOU HAVE A COUNTY COURT JUDGEMENT REGISTERED AGAINST YOU.

How you can pay

Telephone

Our automated payment line is available 24 hours a day on 0845 3000 614.  Please select option 5 for housing benefit overpayment recovery.  You will need your invoice number - «inv_no».  Please note there is a surcharge of 1.65% if you pay by credit card.  There is no surcharge for debit cards.

 

internet

Online payments can be accepted at www.croydon.gov.uk.   Select the “Pay for it” option on the front page and “Housing benefit overpayment” from the list of payment types.  From the Online Payments screen, select “Housing benefit overpayment recovery”.  Please enter your invoice number «inv_no» in the reference number box.  Please note there is a surcharge of 1.65% if you pay by credit card.  There is no surcharge for debit cards.

 

Paypoint

Payments by cash can be made at any outlet displaying the “Paypoint” logo.  You will need your invoice which has a printed bar code unique to you.

 

Post office

You can pay by debit card, cheque or cash at any Post Office although a fee of £2.50 may be charged.  You will need your invoice which has a printed bar code unique to you.

 

At the bank

Payments can be made at any bank, although a fee may be charged.  Please make cheques payable to “Croydon Council” and write your housing benefit reference and invoice number on the reverse of the cheque.  To use this method you will need the giro slip attached to the invoice.

 

BACS or electronic banking

Payments can be transferred via Nat West Bank, account name: LB Croydon, account number: 01000624, sort code: 57-10-70.  The payment should be referenced in the following format 27/invoice number (8 numeric characters only) so your payment can be applied to your invoice.

 

post

Cheques can also be sent to: Croydon Council, Cash & Control, Bernard Weatherill House, 8 Mint Walk, Croydon. CR9 1BQ.  Please enclose your giro slip with your payment.  Cheques should be made payable to “Croydon Council” and include your housing benefit claim reference and invoice number written on the reverse.  Do not send cash through the post.  If you require a receipt, please include the invoice or a letter stating “Please send a receipt”.

 

help & advice about your housing benefit claim or the overpayment

If you need any general advice regarding this overpayment or your benefit claim, you can phone the contact centre on 020 8726 7000.  The opening times are Monday to Friday 8am to 6pm.  E mail: croyhben@croydon.gov.uk  Fax: 020 8667 8440.
